Understand PID

PID control combines three actions to guide process output toward a target setpoint.

P

P - Proportional

Responds to the current error. More Kp usually reduces rise time, but too much can cause overshoot or oscillation.

I

I - Integral

Accumulates past error and removes steady-state offset. Too much Ki can increase overshoot and settling time.

D

D - Derivative

Predicts where the error is heading by reacting to rate of change. More Kd can damp overshoot but can amplify noise.

SP - Setpoint

The target value the loop is trying to reach. If SP is 1.00, the controller keeps adjusting output until the process variable settles near 1.00.

D - Derivative

The rate-of-change term. It reacts to how fast the error is moving, which can slow overshoot and smooth an aggressive response.

PD - Proportional-Derivative

A controller using P and D without integral action. PD responds quickly and adds damping, but it may leave a steady offset if the plant needs integral correction.
